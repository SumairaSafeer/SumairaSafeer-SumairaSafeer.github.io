About

Working at the hardware / software boundary

I'm a Computer Engineering student at COMSATS University Islamabad, Attock Campus, and most of what I build lives at the boundary between a trained model and the chip it has to run on. My final year project redesigned YOLOv8 with CBAM and SE attention plus BiFPN feature fusion, then took that model all the way to a Raspberry Pi with TensorFlow Lite and ONNX — a 75% jump in detection accuracy that only mattered once it also ran in real time.

That end-to-end instinct — model, then hardware, then measurement — carries across everything I do: FPGA and digital logic design, real-time embedded control with FreeRTOS, signal processing over noisy channels, and network infrastructure design. I'm currently extending that range into Generative AI and prompt engineering through concurrent internships at NeuroFive Solutions and Decode Labs.

I'm a full merit-scholarship recipient and a national Prime Minister's Laptop Scheme awardee, currently looking at graduate research programs and engineering roles building intelligent systems that have to earn their keep on real constraints — latency, power, memory — not just on a benchmark.
